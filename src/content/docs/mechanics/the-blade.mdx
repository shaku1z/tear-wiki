The blade is Tear's only weapon and its most complex system. Understanding *how* it moves is the foundation of high-level play. The blade is fully simulated via a spring-physics system.

---

## Physical Properties

| Property | Config Value | Notes |
|----------|--------------|-------|
| **Length** | **95 px** | Physical size from hilt to tip (`blade.length`). |
| **Aim Radius**| **85 px** | The reticle orbits the player within this circle. |
| **Min Tether**| **0.45** | How close the blade is pulled in while held. |
| **Max Reach** | **120 px** | Elastic leash limit before snapping back. |
| **Max Speed** | **7000 px/s** | Absolute hard cap on blade tip velocity. |
| **Lead Amount**| **0.45** | Tip leads ahead of the aim line at high speeds. |

---

## Spring Physics System

The blade does not simply teleport to where you aim. It is attached to the player via a simulated spring.

| Parameter | Value | Effect |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| **Stiffness** | **150** | How aggressively the spring pulls toward target. |
| **Damping** | **7.5** | Resistance to oscillation (prevents infinite bouncing). |
| **Gravity** | **900 px/s²** | Downward pull when idle, giving natural droop. |

When you hold the mouse:
1. The blade is tethered and pulled toward the cursor.
2. Spring tension builds based on `springStiffness`.
3. Upon movement, the tip whips through the arc, constrained by the `maxReach` (120px) leash.

> [!TIP]
> A large, sweeping mouse movement (winding up) produces massive spring velocity on release, dealing max damage. Short, timid flicks won't build enough tension to break the 950 px/s damage threshold.

---

## Throw Mechanics

Pressing right-click releases the blade as a high-speed projectile.

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Base throw speed** | **1900 px/s** |
| **Release bonus** | **+0.45** × tip speed at throw |
| **Max throw speed** | **4600 px/s** |
| **Recall speed** | **3400 px/s** |
| **Max flight time** | **2.5s** (auto-returns) |

Because throw speed includes a bonus from tip speed at release, you can vastly increase throw speed by swinging fast right before releasing. A blade tip moving at 3000 px/s adds **1350 px/s** to the base 1900, launching it at a blistering **3250 px/s**.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The blade auto-recalls after 2.5 seconds. When embedded in an enemy, recalling it rips through their hitboxes (and any enemies in the path) at 3400 px/s.
