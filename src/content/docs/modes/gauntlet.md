Gauntlet mode is the **boss marathon**. It runs like Endless — infinite waves, escalating HP, shop between waves — but adds a mandatory boss encounter every 8 waves. All five campaign bosses rotate in sequence, each cycle harder than the last.

---

## Structure

```
Waves 1–8:   Enemies
Wave 9:      Boss 1 (Ruins)
Waves 10–17: Enemies
Wave 18:     Boss 2 (Canopy)
Waves 19–26: Enemies
Wave 27:     Boss 3 (Depths)
Waves 28–35: Enemies
Wave 36:     Boss 4 (Sanctum)
Waves 37–44: Enemies
Wave 45:     Boss 5 (Apex / Final)
Waves 46–53: Enemies
Wave 54:     Boss 1 (Ruins) — Cycle 2, scaled
...
```

The pattern repeats: **8 enemy waves → boss → 8 enemy waves → next boss** ad infinitum.

---

## Boss Scaling

Each time a boss appears in a later cycle, it receives the same HP scaling as regular enemies — based on the cumulative wave count. By cycle 2 (wave 54+), every boss has significantly more HP than its campaign version.

| Boss Appearance | Approx. HP Multiplier |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| Cycle 1 (waves 9–45) | ×1.0 (baseline) |
| Cycle 2 (waves 54–90) | ~×4.5 |
| Cycle 3 (waves 99–135) | ~×20+ |

Boss move sets and patterns **do not change** between cycles — only HP scales. This means mastery of patterns remains relevant deep into runs; the challenge is sustaining enough damage output to clear escalating HP.

---

## Enemy Waves Between Bosses

Waves between bosses use the same scaling as Endless (+12% HP per wave). The shop operates normally between every wave, including the waves before a boss.

:::tip
**Budget your coins for pre-boss upgrades.** Knowing a boss is coming on wave 9 gives you 8 waves to build. Spend freely in waves 1–6, then save coins from waves 7–8 for a targeted upgrade to counter the incoming boss.
:::

## Boss Rotation Order

| Cycle Position | Boss |
|---------------|------|
| 1st | Boss 1 (Ruins) |
| 2nd | Boss 2 (Canopy) |
| 3rd | Boss 3 (Depths) |
| 4th | Boss 4 (Sanctum) |
| 5th | Boss 5 (Apex) |

Then cycles back to Boss 1 for cycle 2, and so on.

---

## Ability Tier-Ups

Unlike Adventure, Gauntlet does **not** award an Ability Tier-Up on boss kill. The boss is purely a skill challenge — your reward is progress (continuing the run) and coin drops.

---

## Why Play Gauntlet?

Gauntlet occupies a unique niche:

- **Boss practice in context.** Unlike Boss Test (where you enter with a neutral kit), Gauntlet forces you to fight bosses with your current run build — which may be strong or incomplete depending on shop luck. This is the closest simulation to real campaign boss pressure.
- **Pattern consistency.** With 5 bosses every ~45 waves, you see each boss multiple times per long run. Pattern recognition accelerates.
- **Competitive endurance.** Gauntlet runs can be scored similarly to Endless. Reaching late cycles is a mark of mastery over the full boss roster.

---

## Tips

:::tip
**Never skip the shop before a boss wave.** Even a marginal T1 upgrade can make a difference. The boss waves are exactly 8 waves apart, so you always have advance warning.
:::

:::tip
**Practice phase transitions.** Most campaign bosses have a phase change at 50% HP. In Gauntlet cycles 2+, bosses spend more time in high-HP phase 1 before triggering phase 2, giving you more exposure to their phase 1 patterns. Use this to perfect your responses.
:::

:::caution
**Don't over-invest in damage.** Gauntlet's enemy waves are also scaling. A build that one-shots wave 8 enemies may barely tickle them by wave 45. Balance raw damage with status effects and upgrade diversity to maintain viability.
:::
