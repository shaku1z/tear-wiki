Playground and Tutorial are Tear's consequence-free learning spaces. No wave timers, no death, no score pressure — just you and the mechanics.

---

## Playground Mode

**Playground** is a fully open sandbox arena. Everything is accessible immediately:

### Ability Panel

Open the ability menu (in the pause/options overlay) to:
- **Equip any upgrade** from the full list, at any tier.
- **Remove upgrades** freely.
- Combine any set of upgrades that would normally require specific shop rolls to co-exist.

This makes Playground invaluable for:
- Testing upgrade synergies before committing to them in a live run.
- Discovering interactions between status effects (e.g., Rupture T2 + Sunder + MARK).
- Evaluating whether a particular upgrade suits your playstyle before the campaign.

### Enemy Spawn Panel

Open the enemy panel to:
- **Spawn any enemy type** — Charger, Ranged, Flyer, Bomber, Armored, or any boss.
- **Configure enemy affix** — add Tank, Swift, Rapid, Volley, Armed, or Warded manually.
- **Set spawn count** — spawn a single enemy for study or a dozen for stress-testing.

:::tip
To test a specific upgrade interaction, set up the exact scenario: spawn a single Armored enemy, equip Sunder T2, and verify that MARK removes its ground DR. You can't easily do this in a live run.
:::

### Consequences

- No coins are awarded.
- No style meter scores or achievements are tracked.
- No run record is created.
- HP does not regenerate, but you can reset your HP from the panel.

Playground is purely a tool. Use it freely.

## Tutorial Mode

**Tutorial** is a series of linear, guided combat drills. Each drill introduces one mechanic, explains it with on-screen prompts, and gives you a practice scenario to complete before advancing.

### Drill Sequence

| Drill | Mechanic | Goal |
|-------|---------|------|
| 1 | Basic Swing | Hit a target dummy with the blade |
| 2 | Tether & Release | Hold left-click, aim, release for a full swing |
| 3 | Slam | Swing the blade downward at a dummy |
| 4 | Power Slam | Jump, descend, and slam while falling |
| 5 | Launch (Updraft) | Pop a dummy airborne with an upward strike |
| 6 | Juggling | Hit the airborne dummy twice before it lands |
| 7 | Throw | Right-click to throw the blade and hit a target |
| 8 | Recall | Throw the blade, then right-click to recall it through a second target |
| 9 | Deflect | Hit an incoming projectile with the blade |
| 10 | Perfect Parry | Hit a slow projectile at ≥2 400 px/s tip speed |

Each drill displays a tip explaining *why* the mechanic matters — the slam drill, for example, notes the ×1.8 damage multiplier and teaches the downward tip-speed requirement.

### Completion

Completing all 10 drills:
- Unlocks the **Tutorial Complete** record on your profile.
- Does not award achievements or shards on its own.
- Does not need to be completed to access any other mode.

:::note
The Tutorial is optional but strongly recommended for understanding the input geometry of slams and launches. Many players discover the Power Slam mechanic organically in adventure but never learn the exact timing needed to maximize the descent bonus. The drill makes it explicit.
:::

## When to Use Each

| Situation | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| New to the game | Tutorial → then Playground |
| Returning after a break | Playground (refresh combos) |
| Testing a new build idea | Playground |
| Struggling with a boss pattern | Boss Test (for isolated boss practice) |
| Learning a new upgrade | Playground (equip and try) |
